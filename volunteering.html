Parkwood Seniors Community and Other Retirement Homes (2018-Present)

I am a proud long-time volunteer at Parkwood Retirement Home, my local retirement home. I began in 2018 in the cafe, where I served the residents coffee, cookies, and other snacks. I especially loved to talk and converse with the residents and their visitors, and sometimes joined helped out with the different activities and games as well, such as shuffleboard, bingo, and chess. I also played piano occasionally if the atmosphere was a bit quiet or shy. In the cafe, I also trained several new volunteers on what to do. Finally, there were some occasions involving arguments or conflict between residents in which I helped resolve or called a staff member or nurse capable of handling the issue.

In 2020, the lockdown occurred and I found myself unable to return to volunteering. I decided to figure out another way to contribute, and decided to host a virtual piano recital from my home. Originally I was a bit worried it wouldn't go well, but I was told the residents really enjoyed it. Ever since, I have played over 10 recitals and branched out to other retirement homes as far as Goderich! I continue to practice new pieces and perform virtually for the residents.

Additionally, I was invited to join Parkwood's pilot program for JAVAConnects, a music club for retirement home residents. I also participated in a Wilfred Laurier University survey about the program.

Lion Dance (2016-Present)

Ever since I was invited to join the next generation of the lion dance team in KW at the Central Ontario Chinese Cultural Center, I have loved to perform and share my culture through this traditional kung fu demonstration. Although training is exhausting, I still enjoy practicing with my friends and we always have a good time. We attend numerous events including the Multicultural Festival, Lunar New Year Gala, and several restaurant commissions each season. Now I am excited to help train our next lion dance team members and am proud to be able to carry on this cultural tradition. I've also met many new people at events and in the COCCC, which also offers table tennis, dragon dance, dragon boat, and many other services related to Chinese culture.'

Clubs Founded

Chess Club Update: I've running chess club by myself since 2020, and there has been an incredible increase in popularity (likely due to The Queen's Gambit on Netflix). I retrieved the boards that the club owned and we started playing both outdoors in person and online as well. I am super thankful to be able to make everyone's day hopefully more fun and enjoyable with a bit of lunchtime chess! We have over 30 people now and I'm super excited to run the club during my senior year!

In 2019 I cofounded Bluevale Chess Club with two friends. I was always happy to use my graphic design skills to build our posters, and we fundraised money and called several local board games stores, and ended up getting several boards and chess sets. We got to play chess every week! During the pandemic, nearly every club and team was stopped. During this time, I recreated our Bluevale Chess Club online, which has around 25 regular members. This time I did it singlehandedly as my friends were busy and also coping with the pandemic. We gathered on Fridays to play some chess games weekly, couples with a google meet where we could chat about the week, weekend, and games. The club went very well and I was glad I could spread some happiness and fun during the gloomy pandemic. I also created the official Bluevale AP Study Group with my friend and with the help of Bluevale Guidance and teachers to help organize our school's AP registration and create a place where we could look at problems and try to explain understand together. We also met weekly, talking about AP problems and navigating the AP app software. I plan on continuing both clubs in 2021-2022, and look forward to more fun times!

I was very surprised when people started asking me for advice on starting clubs. I offered my encouragement and tips such as getting an announcement spot and using Google Classroom. It was exciting to see many new clubs pop up in our Bluevale community!
