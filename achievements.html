Quantum Cryptography School for Young Students (QCSYS)

Attended QCSYS 2020, a highly selective international program run by the Institute for Quantum Computing. The program teaches students about quantum computing and cryptography, and the exciting possibilities associated with this cutting-edge research.

I was amazed at the applications of linear algebra to quantum mechanics, and learned about bra-ket notation and also did some DIY experiments to calculate Planck's Constant and demonstrate the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle. The lecturers at QCSYS deeply inspired me and I was motivated to continue my lifelong journey of learning.

SHAD

Attended SHAD 2021. SHAD is a month-long STEAM program for selected high school students, filled with lectures and university-level content, as well as the famous design project; the 2021 project was about the protecting our water.
Part of the Western campus

In addition to learning lots about water, water sources, how it cycles through our cities, and problems surrounding water shortages and water quality, SHAD also provided invaluable experience in helping me work in a group. I furthered my teamwork abilities and our group had wonderful collaboration allowing us to develop our cool product. I am now a SHAD Fellow and part of the SHAD Network, and look forward to meeting more friends and people through these resources. PS: The SHAD Project we made is under the "Selected Projects" tab.
